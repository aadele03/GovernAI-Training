Where findings come from
Risk Management findings are generated from three sources: Risk Assessment (when dimension scores exceed thresholds), Vendor Due Diligence (when domain or contract provision gaps are identified), and AI Inventory (when systems are registered with high-risk data types or no human oversight). Findings can also be added manually.

Pathways available

The four pathways are: Mitigate (implement a control), Accept (formally document residual risk), Transfer (shift via contract or insurance), and Avoid (decommission the system or feature). Most findings should be mitigated — accept is a last resort requiring senior sign-off.

Finding sources — where they come from

Every finding in Risk Management originates from one of four sources. The source determines its ID prefix and the recommended remediation approach.

​​💡
The same issue can appear from multiple sources
A data handling risk can generate a finding from the RA (Data sensitivity dimension), a VDD finding (Data Handling domain), and an Inventory finding (PHI flag + no oversight). GovernAI uses a composite deduplication key to prevent the exact same finding appearing multiple times. Each source adds a distinct finding.

⚠️
Critical findings have a 30-day default deadline
GovernAI automatically sets the deadline to 30 days from today for Critical findings, 60 days for High, and 90 days for Medium. These defaults are based on standard governance timelines. You can override them in the detail panel if your policy requires different timelines, but you should document the reason for any extension.
Step 4

Choose a treatment pathway

Every finding must have a pathway assigned before it can be advanced. Choose one of four options based on your organization’s risk appetite.

👆
Try it — select Mitigate for the Critical finding
Click one of the pathway cards below to select it. For the Critical data handling finding on the Churn Predictor, Mitigate is the right choice — we will implement a DPA/BAA and data handling policy. The Accept pathway is only appropriate when residual risk is within your documented risk appetite.
🛡️
Mitigate
Implement a control to reduce the likelihood or impact of the risk. The most common pathway for Critical and High findings.
📝
Accept
Formally document that you are accepting the residual risk. Requires sign-off from the appropriate risk owner. Last resort — not appropriate for Critical findings without board approval.
📋
Transfer
Shift the risk to a third party via insurance, contract indemnification, or outsourcing. The underlying risk remains — you are transferring the financial consequence.
🚫
Avoid
Eliminate the activity, feature, or system generating the risk entirely. Appropriate when the risk cannot be sufficiently mitigated and the business case is weak.
✔
Mitigate selected
Good choice for a Critical finding. The next step is to assign an owner responsible for implementing the control and set a firm deadline. For the data handling finding, the control is: obtain a signed DPA/BAA from all data processors, implement data minimization review, and confirm the sub-processor chain is documented.
When to use each pathway
Mitigate
Default for Critical and High findings. Use when a control exists or can be implemented within the deadline. Most common pathway.
Accept
Use for Low or Medium residual risks after mitigation, or for risks within your documented risk appetite. Requires formal sign-off and rationale. Never use for Critical findings without board approval.
Transfer
Use when the risk can be shifted contractually or via insurance. Common for vendor-generated risks where contractual amendments are feasible.
Avoid
Use when the risk cannot be sufficiently reduced by any other means and the business justification is insufficient. Effectively means shutting down the AI system or feature.

⚠️
Owner must be a named individual, not a team
Assigning “Legal Team” or “Privacy Department” is not sufficient. Regulators will ask who specifically is accountable for each finding. The named owner receives the accountability trail and is the person expected to provide status updates at governance committee meetings.
Step 6

Add notes & attach evidence

Document your remediation plan in notes and attach evidence as controls are implemented. Evidence is your audit trail.

💡
What counts as evidence for each pathway
For Mitigate: upload the implemented control documentation — signed DPA/BAA, updated policy document, penetration test results, configuration screenshot. For Accept: upload the formal acceptance memo signed by the appropriate risk owner. For Transfer: upload the contract amendment or insurance certificate. Evidence attached to findings is version-controlled and appears in the audit trail export.
