Interactive Guide

AI System Registration

Click through every step of the registration workflow. See each field highlighted in the live simulator, understand what it does, and practice filling it in before you use the real application.

5–8 minutes to complete
What you will learn
This guide walks you through all 12 form fields using a live interactive simulator that mirrors the real GovernAI application. You will see exactly where each field appears in the form, understand why it matters for governance, and practice entering values for a real-world example system.
🎯
The governance rule
If an AI system is not registered in the Inventory, it does not exist from a governance standpoint. Every other module — Risk Assessment, Vendor Due Diligence, Risk Management, and the Dashboard — draws its data from systems registered here.
ℹ️
Example system used in this guide
We will register a Customer Churn Predictor — an internally built AI model that scores existing customers on their likelihood to cancel their account, triggering automatic outreach when the score exceeds a threshold. This is a realistic governance scenario that touches most of the form's important fields.

01
System name
Required
Use the official name as it appears in your vendor contract or internal project documentation — not a nickname or abbreviation. This name appears across all six modules and in every report.
Example: Customer Churn Predictor
Inconsistent naming creates confusion during audits. A system called "churn model" in one report and "retention AI" in another creates questions about whether they are the same system.
02
Vendor
Recommended
The name of the company that built or operates the AI system. For internally built systems, enter your organization name or simply "Internal."
Vendor-built: Zest AI  |  Internal: Internal  |  Open source: Internal (Hugging Face)
GovernAI uses this to automatically create a Vendor Due Diligence record. Every unique vendor name across the Inventory gets a corresponding vendor record in the VDD module — even "Internal."
⚠️
Use exact vendor names
If you register one system as "GitHub" and another as "Github (Microsoft)", GovernAI will create two separate VDD vendor records. Use consistent, exact vendor names to keep your vendor register clean.

05
Risk owner
Recommended
The named individual accountable for managing the risk of this AI system. Must be a specific person or role — not a team or committee.
Findings generated from this system are auto-assigned to this owner in Risk Management. Without a named owner, remediation accountability is unclear and regulators will ask who is responsible.
08
Use case
Recommended
A one-to-two sentence description of exactly what this AI does in your environment — not what it could do in general, but the specific way your organization uses it.
Bad: "Uses AI to help with customer decisions."
Good: "Scores existing customers 0–100 on churn likelihood; scores below 30 trigger automatic retention outreach with no human review."
Vague use cases produce inaccurate risk assessments. Be specific about the decision type, the affected population, and the automation level.
Step 4

Regulatory flags

The most consequential part of the form. Each flag activates specific compliance controls and affects how Risk Assessment weights are calculated.

👆
Try it — select the correct flags
For our Customer Churn Predictor, check GDPR / CPRA (processes personal data of customers) and decide whether ECOA / Fair lending applies — it does if the retention offers differ by customer score. Check the guidance below each flag.

Automated employment decisions affecting NYC residents.
⚠️
When in doubt, flag it
Incorrectly flagging a system (over-flagging) creates extra governance work. Not flagging a system that should be flagged (under-flagging) creates a compliance gap that regulators will find. Always err on the side of flagging and resolve the uncertainty with your compliance team.
ℹ️
ECOA and churn scoring — the key question
ECOA applies to credit-related decisions. If your retention team offers different financial incentives (fee waivers, interest rate discounts, credit limit increases) based on the churn score, that constitutes differential credit treatment and ECOA likely applies. If all customers receive identical outreach regardless of their score, ECOA is less likely to apply. Flag it and confirm with your compliance team.

🔑
How these fields affect risk scoring
Financial data (Critical sensitivity) + Human oversight OFF (no human checkpoint) = two of the six risk dimensions will score toward the high end. This Customer Churn Predictor is likely to come out as a Medium or High risk system after formal assessment — even though it might feel low-stakes from a business perspective. That's the governance point: the risk profile reflects the actual exposure, not the perceived importance.

Step 7

What happens automatically

Saving a registration triggers automatic updates across three other modules. You don't need to do anything — the platform does this for you.

⚖️
Risk Assessment
Provisional record created
A Provisional assessment record for SYS-007 appears immediately in the Risk Assessment module — a placeholder showing the system exists but has not been formally scored. The score ring shows blank.
🤝
Vendor Due Diligence
Vendor record auto-created
"Internal" is a new vendor name. A vendor record with Pending status is created automatically. If "Internal" already existed from another system, the new system would link to the existing record.
📊
GRC Dashboard
Metrics update live
The AI Systems card goes from 6 to 7. The Assessments ratio updates to 6/7 (85%). The Analytics department appears in the heatmap. The Vendor Coverage percentage recalculates.
🗄️
AI Inventory
Sequential ID assigned
The system receives SYS-007 — the next sequential ID. This ID appears on all findings, reports, and cross-module references, giving you a stable identifier for the system's entire governance lifecycle.
→
Your next step after registration
Navigate to Risk Assessment and find the new SYS-007 provisional record. Run the four-step formal assessment wizard. This replaces the provisional placeholder with a real scored assessment, populates findings, sets the risk tier, and counts toward your Program Maturity score. This is the highest-value action after registration.
