What the Playbook does
The Governance Playbook is your program’s project plan. It breaks the full NIST AI RMF into 35 concrete tasks across six phases. It tracks completion, stores evidence, and feeds the Program Maturity score on the Dashboard. Complete the Playbook and you have a documented, auditable AI governance program.
​​💡
Start here — before any other module
The GOVERN phase establishes the foundations everything else depends on: your AI risk appetite, governance committee, roles, use policy, incident response procedures, and training requirements. Without these, all downstream governance activity lacks a documented mandate.

⚠️
Don’t skip the GOVERN phase
Regulators conducting AI governance examinations will ask specifically for your AI Risk Appetite Statement, Governance Committee Charter, and AI Use Policy. These are the three documents produced by GOVERN tasks g1, g2, and g4. Without them, your program has no documented mandate.

💡
What counts as evidence?
Upload the actual governance document this task requires — not a summary or note. For the risk appetite task, upload the signed AI Risk Appetite Statement itself. For the governance committee task, upload the committee charter. Files under 2MB are stored within the application. For larger files, add a note in the task description field referencing where the document is stored.
​​⚙️
Auto-completion from other modules
Several Playbook tasks complete automatically when you take actions in other modules. Saving a formal Risk Assessment auto-completes the MAP 2 risk identification task and MEASURE assessment task. Saving a Vendor Due Diligence assessment completes the MAP 2 vendor review task. You don’t need to manually check these off — they update in real time.

Owner must be a named individual, not a team
Assigning “Risk Team” or “Compliance Department” as owner creates ambiguity that regulators and auditors will flag. Each task must have a single named individual at the appropriate seniority level who is personally accountable for the activity being evidenced.

Evidence document guide — GOVERN phase
Risk appetite (g1)
Board-approved AI Risk Appetite Statement with named risk tolerance levels and prohibited use categories. PDF format preferred.
Committee (g2)
Governance committee charter with membership list, terms of reference, meeting cadence, and decision rights. Can be a board resolution or standing committee terms.
Roles (g3)
Org chart or RACI showing AI Owner, AI Risk Officer, and AI Ethics Officer assignments for each registered system.
Use policy (g4)
Published AI Use Policy with board or senior leadership approval memo attached. The policy must address prohibited uses, minimum controls, and employee obligations.
Incident response (g5)
AI incident response runbook with defined escalation paths, notification timelines, and roles. Aligned to your existing IT incident management framework.
Training (g6)
Training policy or requirements document identifying affected staff, frequency, and minimum competency standards. Can be part of the broader AI Use Policy.
