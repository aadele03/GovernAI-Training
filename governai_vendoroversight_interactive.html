🎯
Why vendor oversight matters
Most organizations use AI built by third parties. If a vendor’s model produces biased outputs, suffers a breach, or fails unexpectedly, your organization bears the governance and regulatory consequences. The VDD module gives you a structured, documented way to evaluate every vendor’s AI governance posture and identify contractual gaps before they become incidents.
💡
Vendors are created automatically
You don’t need to manually add vendors. GovernAI reads the Vendor field from every registered AI system and creates a vendor record automatically. Every unique vendor name in your Inventory appears in VDD as a pending record ready for assessment.

Score thresholds
75–100 = Approved — vendor meets minimum governance standards, continue with annual review. 50–74 = Conditional — material gaps exist, use at measured risk while pursuing remediation. Below 50 = Flagged — significant failures, escalate to senior management and consider vendor replacement.

⚠️
Yes requires evidence, not just belief
Yes means you have documented evidence available — a report, certification, test result, or contractual provision you can produce if asked. Partial means some evidence exists but gaps remain. No means the control is not in place. Answering Yes without evidence creates a false picture of vendor governance that will fail under regulatory scrutiny.
Six assessment domains
Model Governance (8)
Model lifecycle management, validation, change control, version tracking, and model cards.
Data Handling (9)
DPA/BAA coverage, training data prohibition, sub-processor disclosure, data residency, encryption, and deletion rights.
Security (10)
SOC 2 Type II, penetration testing, AI-specific attacks (prompt injection, model inversion), vulnerability management.
Transparency (8)
Instance-level explainability, audit trails, model cards, version history, and decision contestability.
Business Continuity (8)
SLAs with financial penalties, tested BCP/DR, geographic redundancy, defined RTO/RPO, and data portability.
Fairness & Bias (10)
Independent disparate impact testing, multiple fairness metrics, production bias monitoring, intersectional bias evaluation.

⚠️
Each missing Required provision = one Critical gap finding
Required provisions that are not present in your contract generate Critical-severity gaps in the scorecard. These gaps flow automatically into the Risk Management register as findings assigned to your vendor record. They should be prioritized for inclusion at the next contract renewal or amendment.

Draft saves all your progress
When you save a draft, GovernAI stores all answers you have given so far, your current wizard step, and a timestamp. The vendor row and detail panel show a blue draft indicator.
