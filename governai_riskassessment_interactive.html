What a risk assessment does
A formal risk assessment converts a Provisional placeholder record into a real scored profile — a number from 0 to 10 across six risk dimensions, a risk tier (Low/Medium/High), an automatically generated set of governance findings, and a calculated next review date. It is the core governance action that creates accountability and drives everything else.
ℹ️
Example system
We will assess the Customer Churn Predictor registered in the previous guide — an internally built model that scores customers on churn likelihood and triggers automatic retention outreach. No human review step exists before action is taken.
💡
Score based on current state
Score each dimension based on how the system operates today — not how you intend to improve it, not what is in the vendor contract. If human review is supposed to happen but rarely does in practice, score for the actual behavior.

Correct answers for the Customer Churn Predictor

Q1
Supports operational processes with moderate downstream impact
The Churn Predictor drives retention outreach decisions, but it doesn't directly determine individual rights or financial outcomes the way a credit underwriting model does. It is operational — the consequence is operational disruption or missed revenue, not adverse action on an individual's legal rights.
Q2
External individuals — customers
The system scores actual customers. Even though the outreach decision is internal, the population affected is external customers who may receive (or miss receiving) retention offers based on the AI's output.
Q3
Medium — operational disruption, reputational risk, or recoverable financial loss
If the model systematically misclassifies at-risk customers, the company loses revenue and customer relationships. This is recoverable but significant. It rises to High if the retention offers constitute credit products regulated under ECOA.
Q4
No formal validation — deployed without structured assessment
The system was built and deployed internally without a structured validation process. This is the honest answer for many internally built models. It is critical to record this accurately — it will generate a finding in the results step.

Understanding the score

1–3

The score is a weighted average: Decision impact × 22% + Data sensitivity × 20% + Regulatory exposure × 20% + Human oversight × 15% + Third-party dependency × 13% + Model transparency × 10%.

Where the findings come from

Critical
Sensitive data processing without documented controls
Data sensitivity ≥ 7
Financial and PII data scored 8/10 — above the Critical threshold of 7. The recommended control: verify BAA/DPA coverage, implement data minimization review, and confirm the sub-processor chain. This finding will appear in Risk Management automatically.
High
Automated decisioning with no human checkpoint
Human oversight ≥ 7
Human oversight scored 8/10 — the system triggers outreach automatically with no human review. Recommended control: implement sampling-based human review and automated exception flagging for anomalous outputs.
High
Regulatory compliance gap not formally assessed
Regulatory exposure ≥ 6
Regulatory exposure scored 6/10 — the system operates under GDPR/CPRA obligations that have not been formally assessed. Recommended control: run a compliance gap assessment and remediate priority gaps within 60 days.

What happens after saving

Saving a formal assessment triggers automatic updates across four modules — and starts the governance clock for this system.

🗄️
AI Inventory
Risk tier and status updated
SYS-007 changes from Medium/Pending to High/Approved. The system's NIST functions, next review date, and findings list are all updated from the assessment results.
🛡️
Risk Management
3 findings appear automatically
Click "Send findings to Risk Management →" in the detail panel to push all three findings into the active register. Each gets a CTRL- ID, a default owner, and a recommended deadline.
📋
Governance Playbook
Two tasks auto-complete
The MAP 2 risk identification task and the MEASURE system assessment task are marked complete automatically — without any manual action in the Playbook.
📊
GRC Dashboard
Metrics and maturity update
The Assessments ratio improves (7/7 = 100%). The Program Maturity score rises because AI Systems Assessed component is now fully complete. The activity trail logs the assessment completion.
